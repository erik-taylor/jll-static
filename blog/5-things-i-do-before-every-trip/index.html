5 Things I do BEFORE Every Trip

By Jetlag Leah • Monday, 11 February, 2019

Preparing for a trip can be stressful, there is so much to do and if you wait until the last minute like I do, very little time to do it. Every trip has a unique checklist but here are the top 5 things I do before every trip to lower my stress levels prior to the trip and to make my return a little more relaxing.

  1. Laundry – I do at least one load of laundry before every trip, not only to assure that all the clothes I wanted to pack are clean but also to lessen the amount of laundry I will need to when I return. Pro Tip- put a dryer sheet in your luggage to keep everything smelling nice, even at towards the end of your trip when your dirty clothes outnumber the clean.

  2. Clean the messiest room in the house- I am usually a very clean and organized person but when I pack, I blow through my house like a tornado! I gather everything I think I might need and throw it on my bed. This usually leaves almost every room in my house in various stages of disarray. Cleaning the messiest room is just one less thing I have to do when I get home and it gives me a place to sit for a minute if I get overwhelmed while packing.

5 things I do before every trip to lower my stress levels

  1. Put fresh sheets on the bed- The day I leave, not matter how early or late my departure, I always put fresh sheets on my bed. I think we can all agree the first night in a bed with clean sheets is incredible. After a couple days/weeks/months of sleeping in beds that are not yours, that first night back in your bed, with clean sheets is nothing less than euphoric.

  2. DIY Spa- although I usually stress out the night before a trip, doing laundry and cleaning my house, packing, double and triple checking the weather in my destination to make sure I packed the right clothes and packing and repacking my bag so everything fits just right. I also try to take some time to pamper myself too. I do a facemask, a hair conditioning treatment, a foot scrub, paint my nails or just slather my body in lotion. Anything and everything to help me distress and feel like I am putting my best foot forward at the start of my trip.

  3. Gather all the info- I make sure all of my flight, housing, car, identification and entertainment information is readily available on my phone as well as printed out in my bag. While having all the information on your phone is convenient, it is really important to have hard copies incase the unthinkable happens and you are without access to that information on your phone. What do you do to prepare the night before a trip?
